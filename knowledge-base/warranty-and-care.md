# Warranty & Gear Care — Trailhead Outfitters

## Our warranty
- Trailhead Outfitters products carry a **1-year limited warranty** against **manufacturing
  defects** in materials and workmanship, from the date of purchase.
- The warranty covers things like seams coming apart, broken zippers/buckles, delamination, or
  faulty stitching.
- It does **not** cover normal wear and tear, accidental damage, improper care, or the natural
  breakdown of materials over time.

## How to make a warranty claim
1. Ask to **talk to a human** in chat, or mention that an item is **defective/damaged**.
2. Have your **order number (TO-#####)** and a short description (a photo helps).
3. We'll review and, if it's a covered defect, **repair, replace, or refund** it — even if it's
   **outside the 30-day return window**.

## Damaged on arrival
If something shows up damaged, don't send it back yet — contact us first (keep the packaging).
We'll arrange a replacement or refund right away.

## Gear care

### Down jacket (Aurora Down Jacket)
- Wash sparingly on a **gentle cycle with a down-specific detergent**; rinse twice.
- **Tumble dry low with two or three clean tennis balls** to re-loft the down. Make sure it's
  fully dry before storing.
- Store **loosely**, not compressed.

### Merino base layer (Basecamp Merino)
- Machine wash **cold, gentle**, with like colors. **Lay flat or hang to dry.**
- Skip fabric softener and bleach. Merino resists odor, so it needs washing less often.

### Fleece (Cedar Fleece Pullover)
- Machine wash **cold**, tumble dry low. Avoid fabric softener (it coats the fibers).

### Rain jacket (Summit Rain Jacket)
- Wash with a **technical wash** when it gets dirty (dirt reduces water repellency).
- To revive beading, **reactivate the DWR finish** with low heat (tumble dry low or a warm iron
  on the lowest setting with a cloth between).

### Tents (Ridgeline, Basecamp)
- **Dry completely** before storing to prevent mildew. Sweep out grit, spot-clean with mild
  soap and water. Never machine wash. Store loosely in a cool, dry place.

### Sleeping bags (Nimbus, Glacier)
- Store **uncompressed** in the large mesh sack, not the stuff sack — long-term compression
  ruins down loft. Wash rarely, with down-specific detergent.

### Hiking socks & pants
- Machine wash cold, tumble dry low. Turn socks inside out to clear trail grit.
