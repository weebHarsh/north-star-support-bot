# Sizing Guide — Trailhead Outfitters

## How to measure
- **Chest:** measure around the fullest part of your chest, under the arms, keeping the tape level.
- **Waist:** measure around your natural waistline (roughly at the belly button).
- **Inseam:** measure from the crotch seam to the bottom of the ankle.
Use a soft tape measure and keep it snug but not tight.

## Tops & jackets (XS–XXL)
Fits jackets, fleece, base layers, sun hoodies.

| Size | Chest (in) | Waist (in) |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| XS   | 33–35     | 27–29     |
| S    | 35–37     | 29–31     |
| M    | 38–40     | 32–34     |
| L    | 41–43     | 35–37     |
| XL   | 44–46     | 38–41     |
| XXL  | 47–49     | 42–45     |

**Fit notes**
- **Base layers** (Basecamp Merino) are designed to fit close to the skin — pick your true size.
- **Midlayers & insulated jackets** (Cedar Fleece, Aurora Down) leave room to layer — if you
  plan to wear thick layers underneath, size up.
- **Rain shells** (Summit Rain Jacket) are a regular athletic fit over a midlayer.
- **Between two sizes?** Size up for layering pieces and outerwear; stay true-to-size for base
  layers.

## Hiking pants (waist sizing)
Trailblazer Hiking Pants use numeric **waist sizes 28–40** with a regular inseam and a bit of
stretch. Order the waist size you'd buy in everyday pants; the convertible legs and stretch
waistband handle small variations.
